About

Household use of air conditioners (ACs) is growing steadily in Canada (source). In Canada, air conditioners contribute the most to summer energy demaind out of all household appliances. This is because they generally have a low energy efficiency. By using our calculator, you can choose a more efficient AC. Or you can skip the AC and open some windows. These actions will reduce energy demand and your carbon footprint. You will also save money on your electricity bills. To quantify exactly much money you could save, use our cost calculator.

How the calculator works

The calculator collects data from several sources to create a personalized cost estimate. The following formula is used to calculate the cost:

Cost of operation = (24 x DDc·18 / TOD - 18) x (CAP (35°C) / 0.9 EER) x (Cost/kW/1000)

where
  • DDc·18 is number of cooling degree-days (base 18°C), i.e. the number of days when the temperature exceeds 18 °C;
  • TOD is the summer outdoor design temperature (°C) for the user-provided location;
  • CAP (35°C) is the capacity of the AC (in Btu/h) at an entering air temperature of 35°C;
  • EER is the rated energy efficiency ratio; and,
  • Cost per kWh is the local electricity cost (in $/kWh).

For more information on this equation and ways to air condition your home efficiently visit NRCan’s air conditioner information page.
